Networking & Connections

The Career Development Office is Here to Help You Network with Ease

Building connections in your career is incredibly important and UMSI's CDO has compiled all the best resources for you to succeed. Alumni, professors and even friends can all be a part of your network!

Get Course Credit to Network Prep

Both undergraduate and graduate students can take courses towards their degrees in career development. SI 300 is a 1 credit undrgraduate course required for BSI students and SI 505 is an optional career and internship studio for 1st year MSI students. There's also opportunities to get credit by completing an internship. Learn more in the video below!

Ways To Network

  1. Cold email: Send a polite and professional message to a professional in your area of interest.LinkedIn is a great place to find alumni in your field.
  2. Career Fairs: Companies are at career fairs to talk to you! Bring your resume and show your enthusiasm in their work.
  3. Professional Organizationd on Campus: The University of Michigan has many student orgs focused on providing carreer resources such as professional workshops,alumni events and more.
  4. Coffee Chats:Schedule a time to informally meet in person or virtually to learn more about someone's work and the industry.

Networking can certainly be intimidating but there are tons of resources specially currated by UMSI to guide you. Some of our favorite resources are below, but also check out our Canvas page for even more!
